Variations of the Argentine Gyre observed by GRACE and Ocean Altimetry

The Argentine Gyre is a persistent, energetic mesoscale circulation in the south Atlantic Ocean. We study its nonseasonal variability by analyzing data from the satellite mission GRACE that measures the time-variable gravity, in conjunction with the ocean altimetry and two ocean general circulation model outputs. We Found that the Argentine Gyre undulates up-and-down in sea level variation temporally in pace with the Antarctic Oscillation, which is not verified before. We also confirm the existence of a high-frequency rotary oscillation within the gyre at ~25-day periodicity. We demonstrate that GRACE is sensitive enough to detect oceanographic signals at spatial and temporal resolutions higher than practiced hitherto.

This work was done when I was visiting the Academia Sinica in Taipei during my master's study. It was the first time that I got to know a little bit about ocean science.

Relevent paper: Yu, Y., Chao, B. F., García‐García, D., & Luo, Z. (2018). Variations of the Argentine Gyre observed in the GRACE time‐variable gravity and ocean altimetry measurements. Journal of Geophysical Research: Oceans, 123(8), 5375-5387. [pdf]
